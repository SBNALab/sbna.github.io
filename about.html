Leading Center of Social, Medical and Biological Network Analysis

Welcome! We are a multidisciplinary research lab at the computer engineering department of the University of Kurdistan, located in the beautiful city of Sanandaj in western Iran. Our focus is on network analysis research in various fields, including social, medical, and biological networks.

Our Mission

Our aim is to create models of networks that encompass social, medical, and biological systems, enabling us to understand, anticipate, address, and propose solutions for interconnected issues while taking into account the connections between various entities. By bridging the realms of social and biological knowledge, we can exchange experiences and expertise to uncover novel research perspectives and generate fresh insights.

Research Scope

We are an active multidisciplinary group at the computer engineering department of the University of Kurdistan. Our lab members come from different majors, and our focus is on network analysis research in various fields. Furthermore, we conduct practical analyses of social and biological networks for nationwide and international customers. The level of network analysis can vary from the micro-level, such as living organism genes, to the macro-level, such as relationships between people, groups, or companies.

Active Projects

Bipartite Network Analysis

Bipartite graphs are a special type of graphs where relations are only permitted between nodes of different parts. In other words, the connections between parts are important, not connections within each part. Some examples include gene-disease, student-course, country-product, drug-gene, and more. Modeling and general calculations on such networks require different equations and considerations. We are currently working on two different aspects of bipartite networks: theory and applications. For theory, we are concentrating on improving popular network analysis tasks such as bipartite link prediction, bipartite community detection, converting related time series to bipartite graphs for further analysis, and more. For applications, we have several ongoing research projects, including better drug-target interaction modeling, educational process improvement, global trade connection predictions for various fields, and more.

Network-based Time Series Analysis

A large part of data gathered from biological, medical, economical, and other fields are in the form of time series. Examples include EEG signals, price time series, climate statistics, and more. Various computational tasks have been performed to predict or enhance such data, with the majority of them based on traditional machine learning techniques. However, a network and relational view of these time series data may open new horizons for analysis and mining. Therefore, we are currently focused on visibility graph analysis topics and progressively extending its application to new areas.
