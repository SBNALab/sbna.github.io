Pioneering Research

At SBNA Lab, we stand at the forefront of computational research, unraveling the complexities of interconnected systems through the synergy of graph theory, machine learning, and data analysis. Our work spans from the intricacies of social networks to the dynamics of biological systems, all unified by our commitment to understanding and harnessing the power of networks.

Our Research Areas

Graph Theory & Networks

We apply graph theory to model and analyze complex networks, from social interactions to biological pathways. Our research includes network analysis, community detection, and graph algorithms.

Machine Learning

We develop and apply cutting-edge machine learning algorithms for network analysis, predictive modeling, and pattern recognition in both social and biological contexts.

Data Analysis

Our team specializes in extracting meaningful insights from large-scale, complex datasets. We use advanced statistical methods and visualization techniques to uncover hidden patterns.

Link Prediction

We predict future connections in networks, whether it's friendships in social media or interactions in protein networks, using a blend of graph theory and machine learning.

Artificial Intelligence

Our AI research focuses on creating intelligent systems that can understand and navigate complex network structures, with applications in recommendation systems and autonomous agents.

Software Development

We develop robust, scalable software tools and libraries that empower researchers and industries to analyze and leverage network data effectively.

Featured Research Projects

Community Detection in Social Networks

We've developed a novel algorithm that outperforms existing methods in identifying communities within large-scale social networks. Our approach, which combines spectral clustering with a reinforcement learning model, has been validated on Twitter and Facebook datasets, revealing insights into the formation and evolution of online communities.

Link Prediction in Biological Networks

Our recent work in computational biology focuses on predicting protein-protein interactions. By integrating graph neural networks with domain-specific features, we've achieved a 15% improvement in prediction accuracy over state-of-the-art methods, accelerating drug discovery and understanding of cellular processes.
